A Bangkok institution that's been on Mahachai Road since 1966. The queue snakes past rows of woks blasting flame at midnight. Order the *Pad Thai haw khai* — wrapped in a thin egg net, delivered on a small plate with a wedge of lime, bean sprouts, and dried chilli.

The ritual is the point: stand in the street, unfold the omelette wrapper with chopsticks, squeeze the lime, watch the condensation bead on the plastic cup of iced jasmine tea.

They close when the noodles run out, which can be as early as 1 am on a busy night. Come after 10 pm if you want to avoid the worst of the tourist crush — the locals arrive late.
