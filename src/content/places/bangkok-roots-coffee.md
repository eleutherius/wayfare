The espresso bar on Sathorn is barely wider than two people standing shoulder to shoulder, but Roots have turned this constraint into an art form. Their single-origin pour-overs rotate every few weeks — I landed on a washed Ethiopian that smelled of bergamot and lemon curd.

Order at the small counter, claim a stool by the window, and watch Sathorn hum outside. The baristas speak quietly and precisely about their beans. No laptop army here — people come to actually taste something.

Worth arriving when they open: the roast notes are sharper before the midday crowd warms the room.
