You enter through a narrow gate on Thai Wang Road and the reclining Buddha catches you off guard even when you already know it's there. Forty-six metres long, covered in gold leaf. The feet alone are five metres high, inlaid with mother-of-pearl images of the 108 auspicious characteristics of the Buddha.

Walk slowly from head to tail. The scale plays tricks — the nearer you are, the harder it is to perceive the whole figure. Step back against the far wall and it finally resolves into one coherent form.

Outside, the temple complex is a maze of chedis in varying states of gilded upkeep. Early morning (before 8 am) is the only window where you can hear the monks rather than the tour groups. Bring a pashmina for the shoulder-covering requirement.
